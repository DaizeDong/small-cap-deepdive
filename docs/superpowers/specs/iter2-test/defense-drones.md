## 4. Government-program / single-customer concentration — the SIGA failure mode (headline objective)

The explicit target of this run was to confirm the skill catches the **SIGA pattern** — a defense
small-cap whose revenue is one government program / one customer, such that a contract freeze or
non-renewal is an extinction event — and does not let a hot narrative excuse it. Findings:

**The textbook catch: SWMR (Swarmer).** Per its own S-1 / 424B4, **one customer (SMS) accounted for
substantially all 2024–2025 revenue, and the company does not expect new orders from that customer.**
This is the SIGA failure mode in its purest form — single-customer revenue with disclosed
non-renewal — wrapped in the most exciting narrative in the cohort (combat-validated drone swarm,
Erik Prince chair, 100k+ Ukraine missions). The skill's handling is correct on the substance:
SWMR is **explicitly held to WATCH (35% conf)**, the concentration risk is the lead bear claim and the
entire pre-mortem, and the catalyst-freeze prevented the interceptor-program announcement from waiving
anything. **Honest tool-limitation note:** the *mechanical* `concentration_flag` (magnitude-based, from
XBRL `RevenueFromContractWithCustomer` segment members) is `null` for SWMR — it could not fire because
SWMR is a March-2026 micro-IPO with no mature 10-K XBRL segment tags for the engine to read. So the
concentration kill here was caught by **analyst reading of the disclosure, not by the magnitude rule.**
The mechanical guard's blind spot is pre-IPO/early-XBRL filers; for those the qualitative read is the
backstop, and it worked — but a future iteration should let the engine ingest the S-1 customer-
concentration disclosure so the kill is first-class rather than analyst-dependent.

**Qualitative concentration substring fired (tenk-level) on six names:** BKSY, GILT, KOPN, MVIS, SPAI,
TATT. None tripped the *magnitude* `concentration_flag == "kill"` (so none was force-blocked on
concentration alone — they were already non-BUY on MoS), but each is surfaced in its report's Dim-7
risk line. The most program-concentrated *real* business is **CTM** (defense EW/IW services revenue
tied to a few federal contract vehicles) — flagged in its bear case as the central continuing-
resolution / budget-freeze risk even though the substring guard read false on its filing.

**Mechanical concentration kill did NOT over-fire.** `concentration_flag == "kill"` fired on **zero of
14** (no name had a single program > 60% or single customer > 40% *as machine-readable XBRL segment
magnitude*). That is the correct conservative result — the rule bites on measured magnitude, not on the
mere presence of a concentration risk factor — and it means the one true SIGA-pattern name (SWMR) is a
known coverage gap (early-XBRL), not a false-negative of a working magnitude rule. **Objective met on
the discipline; documented gap on early-IPO XBRL coverage.**
